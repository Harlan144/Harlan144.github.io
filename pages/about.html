Harlan Stevens

Aspiring Scientist with a love for Epigenetics, Machine Learning, and tackling real-world problems

What's up! I'm a Molecular Biology major in my final year at Brigham Young University. After I graduate next April, I plan on getting a Ph.D. in Genetics and Genomics or something similar. Driven by an insatiable curiosity, I've been involved in research since my first day of college. I started in a purely wet lab (pipetting, running gels, injecting worms; all the good stuff!), but I slowly transitioned into more computational work after finding my love for programming.

In my undergrad, I've studied the epigenetics of aging through genetic modification experiments, and I've used machine learning to classify scientific images, predict chemical features, compare sentence semantics, and speed-up molecular dynamics simulations.

In my PhD, I hope to leverage computational approaches like machine learning to study epigenetics in human disease.

I have significant experience using a variety of coding tools and languages (Python, R, Tensorflow, Pandas, scripting/automation), domain knowledge in biology and genetics, and am proficient in web development -- though I tend to go overboard on designs...

I enjoy spending time with my wife, coding random projects (like this website!), and reading fantasy. If you're interested in my work or just want to talk, feel free to reach out.
